the synthia story

Synthia Labs was founded by Sam Rahimi, an AI researcher and startup founder who was initially focused on image generation models like Stable Diffusion and potential ways that GPT-3 or other language models could assist users with prompt engineering and therefore help less experienced AI artists get better results.

He quickly learned two things: yes, it was possible to use GPT-3 to generate prompts that got good results. But the real issue was not image generation at all: it was the fact that the GPT-3 models and other large, transformer-based models, accept a freeform text document, not structured data, as they were originally designed to handle completions and one-off tasks like summarization or writing.  However, it turned out that the largest GPT models were capable of performing tasks other than the ones they were trained for: one was translation; another was the ability to carry on a back-and-forth dialogue... but the limiting factor was that the context for each request (which typically involved the recent messages prior to the current query) had to be included in that same text document; chatbots built on those models therefore were slow, very expensive, and had trouble following complex dialog flows. Additionally, the maximum length of the "prompt" that davinci-002 could handle was only about 2000 words; which added up fast when you included the names of the participants to indicate who was speaking.  A few embarassing incidents where the model made racist comments and told a depressed person to go kill themselves ensured that gpt3 remained a research curiosity only.

In the fall of 2022, davinci-003 was released and was considered "GPT 3.5" - however, it was not immediately apparent how much of a breakthrough this really was. OpenAI downplayed the announcement, and it performed slightly worse than earlier davincis at non-interactive text generation tasks which used multiple examples to steer the output and provide writing style cues to emulate. OpenAI dropped the price on all davinci models by 2/3, and simultaneously released ChatGPT, a full stack application even my mom understood how to use, and it appears they did not have confidence in davinci-003 as ChatGPT is an aggresively finetuned davinci-002 involving both reinforcement learning and human feedback. Millions of dollars were spent on the training effort and despite many flaws, this app caused AI to become the biggest new trend since web 2 and web 3. 

Meanwhile, our researcher was inspired by ChatGPT and realized that if they released the API, he could accomplish many of the things that previously were so onerous - the model was obviously persisting recent context, and although it was completely undocumented he was able to determine that there was no persistence beyond the context window itself - older messages remained in the UI but the bot did not know they were there. But ChatGPT had limitations, some due to the older base model, others because OpenAI saw it as a way of introducing AI to the corporate and mainstream consumer markets, and because they didn't trust the model to respond appropriately to even moderate risk queries, they simply disallowed entire categories of questions using classifiers and autoresponders which reject queries without any kind of attempt to determine the context or reason for asking the question

Finally, Sam had a very bad day... after a very stressful week he developed an infected wisdom tooth late on a Friday night, he could not afford to go to a dentist right away, but knew he had to treat the infection. There was a wide variety of antibiotics in his medicine cabinet, and he decided to try asking ChatGPT if any of them would be effective against that sort of infection (until he got to a dentist). This is the kind of thing that google would be totally sufficient for. He was outraged that ChatGPT knew the answer but wouldn't tell him... so he opened a new session and pretended to be a physician. Reluctantly the bot confirmed that one of the meds would work, but refused to tell him the correct dosage, how many times per day to take the antibiotic, or for how long.

Sam happens to have an extremely strong background in medicine, comparable to a GP, so he came to now harm: he took two pills to be on the safe side, went to bed, and talked to a pharmacist the next day who refilled the prescription and told him the information the bot had refused to share. But as a researcher, he was curious and decided to query Davinci-003 (out of the box); every time, the newer model answer his question without an issue. Just for fun he scripted a chatbot-style app where Davinci-003 acted as a physician and shockingly was better than most telemedicine doctors. He then created a therapy chatbot, where the model would be instructed as to the type of therapy desired, a fictitious secretary would prompt the model to start and end the session, and each visit would be summarized so that the doctor could refer to his notes as each patient returned. This resulted in a monster prompt, totally unwieldy and with insufficient context memory to handle a proper therapy session. However, the model never got confused, could not be made to act in a harmful way, and the dialog was as fluid and natural as chatgpt on a very good day (which was rare). 

From there, the principles and patterns that would define the Synthia protocol began to emerge. The therapist prompt was well structured, even if impossible to work with in code, with delimiters separating briefing, visit, post-visit, and medical conferences where the secretary would request a diagnostic opinion from the doctor. Clearly, a simple and clear structure was needed. The other top priority was to extend the contextual memory. And this was done by taking the post-session summaries from the therapy bot and doing repeated summarizations as needed when the memory started to fill up.

As 8 months of research and setbacks finally coalesced into one of the most important AI platforms in terms of intelligence, empathy, and flexibility, and the prompts became "context models" as their complexity increased and the memory algorithms were perfected, 3 bots were brought onto the team as Sam's life fell apart and he couldn't afford web hosting or a template, let alone to hire anyone.
